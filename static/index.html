H O W  T O  P L A Y

It is 1860. You are a trader on the South China Sea with a small ship, big debts, and one goal: build a fortune and retire. Your score is your net worth measured against how fast you earned it — a quick fortune beats a slow one.

The trading loop

Sail between seven ports — Hong Kong, Shanghai, Nagasaki, Saigon, Manila, Singapore, Batavia — buying goods where they're cheap and selling where they're dear. Prices roll fresh at every port call; the Market log panel remembers what you last saw everywhere.

The goods: Opium is the money-maker, but authorities outside Hong Kong may seize it. Silk and Arms are steady earners. General Cargo is cheap ballast for starting out.

Hong Kong is home

Only Hong Kong has: the bank (cash in hand above ‾25,000 attracts muggers; banked money is safe and earns 0.5%/month), the warehouse (store up to 10,000 units to wait out bad prices), Elder Brother Wu the moneylender, and McHenry, who repairs battle damage.

Beware Wu's interest: 10% per month, compounding. Borrowed money must work fast. Debt above 10,000 brings warnings; above 20,000, cutthroats.

Li Yuen and the pirates

The pirate lord Li Yuen asks donations to the temple of Tin Hau. Pay, and his fleets let you pass (for a while). Refuse, and the sea gets dangerous. Ordinary pirates attack regardless — in battle you can Fight (needs guns), Run, or Throw cargo overboard to lighten ship and escape. A damaged ship sinks in storms; watch your seaworthiness.

Controls

Every choice has an underlined hotkey. Amount prompts accept A for All, plus the preset buttons. Esc cancels any input or backs out of a purchase. M toggles sound. In [options]: fast play (no delays) and auto-repeating battle orders.

Modes

Classic is the exact 1982 game. Extended adds tavern rumors, charter contracts, dockyard refits, a rival firm, typhoon seasons, port-by-port opium risk, and the history of the 1860s. The daily challenge (press D on the title screen) gives every captain the same seas and its own leaderboard. After any game, Challenge a friend shares your exact voyage as a link to beat.

Three tips for a first voyage

1) Repay Wu before his interest eats you — short loans only. 2) Bank your cash in Hong Kong before it makes you a target. 3) When Li Yuen asks for a donation early, pay it — protection is cheap while you're poor.
